The Cleveland Indians could not turn a fine performance from right fielder Elliot Boling into a win. Instead, the Memphis Blues prevailed, 6-5. Boling went 3 for 4 with 2 doubles and a walk, while adding 3 RBI and scoring once. The second-place Blues lifted their record to 64-50.

The game was at a stalemate until catcher Augusto Bernabe came to bat in the bottom of the eighth. Facing Cleveland pitcher Tatum Rousseau, he hit a 3-1 fastball for a run-scoring single. It was a game-changer, putting Memphis on top, 6-5.

"We can always improve and get better, but we're feeling confident after that win," said Bernabe.
