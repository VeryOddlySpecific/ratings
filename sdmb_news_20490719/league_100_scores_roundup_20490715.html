NORFOLK ATLANTICS AT PHILADELPHIA HORNETS — 4-7

Davis Stars as Hornets Top Atlantics, 7-4

The 7-4 Philadelphia win over Norfolk was due in large part to Jack Davis. The Hornets left fielder went 3 for 5 with a home run and 2 doubles, while scoring 3 and driving in 3. Joe Withers was credited with the save. He has now successfully converted 32 of 34 save opportunities. With the win, Philadelphia pushed its record to 52-37.

Davis had a game-changing double in the bottom of the sixth. On a 1-2 changeup from James Perkins, he banged a 2-run double to right, giving the Hornets a 5-4 lead. It was the 17th double of the year for Davis.

"Very good win for our guys," said Davis.

ANTELOPE VALLEY RAPTORS AT HUDSON VALLEY RENEGADES — 1-4

Antelope Valley Defeated By Renegades, 4-1

The Hudson Valley Renegades topped the Antelope Valley Raptors, 4-1, at Old League Park. Hudson Valley first baseman Jesús Canales was a spark plug for the offense. He went 2-2 with a double and 2 walks. The winner in relief was Javier Martínez. The left-hander allowed no runs on 1 hit in 3.2 innings. He now owns a record of 6-4. The win puts the Renegades at 41-48, while the Raptors drop to 44-47.

The Renegades got a big at-bat from catcher Brett Hobbs. For the game, Hobbs went 1 for 4 with a double, while adding 2 RBI. In the bottom of the first, with runners on 1st and 2nd, he stroked a 2-run double. His 17th double of the season gave Hudson Valley a 2-0 lead.

"I just try to go out and give it 100 percent every game," Canales told a local reporter after the game. "That's my mantra, and like today, it will pay off more often than not."

NEW ORLEANS CRAWDADDY'S AT WASHINGTON PATRIOTS — 2-6

Crawdaddy's Popped by Patriots, 6-2

Getting offensive support from Chuck Davis, the Washington Patriots topped the New Orleans Crawdaddy's, 6-2. Davis belted 2 home runs, while adding 3 RBI and scoring twice for the Patriots. The Crawdaddy's have now dropped 5 straight.

Davis had a game-changing home run in the bottom of the seventh. On a full count cutter from Marvin Williams, he banged a 2-run home run to right, giving the Patriots a 4-2 lead. It was the 15th home run of the year for Davis.

Washington manager Mitch Logue commended his team for "doing what it takes to win."

SAN DIEGO GROOT AT TORONTO BEAVERS — 8-7

Groot Take Down Beavers, 8-7

San Diego and Toronto duked it out and the Groot came out victorious.

Ralph Randall made an impact in the 8-7 San Diego win over Toronto. Randall, who is tied for 8th in the ZL in extra base hits with 40, was 2 for 6 with 2 home runs. He drove in 3 runs and scored twice. San Diego now has a record of 63-27 with the win.

The game was at a stalemate until left fielder Cory Collins came to bat in the top of the 12th. Facing Toronto pitcher Clarence Murphy, he hit a 1-2 sinker for a run-scoring single. It was a game-changer, putting San Diego on top, 8-7.

"Winning never gets dull," said Randall.

Black Crackers Lose at Home, 6-3

CHIHUAHUA DIVISIÓN DEL NORTE AT COLORADO PEAKS — 6-7

Chihuahua Loss Tarnishes Nuncio Performance

The Colorado Peaks besoiled an otherwise fine performance by Chihuahua center fielder José Nuncio by beating the División del Norte, 7-6, at Elysian Fields. Nuncio slugged 3 home runs. He drove in 4 runs and scored three times. Colorado pitcher Luis Mejía got the win, upping his record to 2-3. Jack Mills got the loss. The triumph puts Colorado at 49-41 for the season.

Colorado took a 7-6 lead on Joaquín Hernández's 2nd triple of the season. The 2-run triple, which Hernández hit in the bottom of the eighth, left Chihuahua pitcher Steve Lehman visibly frustrated.

"Nice win for us," said Hernández. "Now we'll go after the next one."
