The Chihuahua División del Norte couldn't capitalize on the big game from Nuncio and dropped the contest, 7-6.

The mood in the División del Norte clubhouse afterward was subdued, with several players remarking that they had allowed the Peaks to spoil the day for Nuncio.

"Some of the boys are beating themselves up about it," Chihuahua manager Alexander Steadman said. "They know we win as a team and lose as a team, but it's hard to really put that idea into practice on a day when one of you was playing on such an obviously higher level."

In addition to the homers, Nuncio collected 4 RBIs and scored 3 runs.

"I got three pitches down the pipe and popped them," the Chihuahua center fielder told reporters. "Today I was in a groove. Tomorrow I could just pop the same pitches up and go 0-for-3."

José Nuncio hit a two-run home run off Dave Andrews in the 2nd, hit a solo-shot off Dave Andrews in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Dave Andrews in the 7th and flied out in the 8th.

So far this season, Nuncio has put up a .289 batting average with 88 hits, 16 home runs and 54 RBIs.
